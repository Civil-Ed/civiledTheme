About Us

We're CivilScope, a Digital Social Database startup targeting Sudanese youth Initiatives, Grassroots movements and start-up social enterprises.

Our goal is pretty simple, we believe in youth power and we want to advance it through a digital civic infrastructure. That means providing the necessary tools, networks and expertise and resources for social actors to amplify their impact.

Our platform acts through a social media like format in mapping social change actors while providing a one-stop online resources center for everyone you might want to work with, everything you need to learn, and every opportunity you can take - All produced and added by the Community of social actors themselves. Thus creating an ecosystem of digital interactions and collaborations that automatically maps actors and archives their posts, reports and shared content. (We've published our beta Prototype on CivilScope.org/en).

Our Idea

CivilEd.Org, a Massive Open Online Courses (MOOC) Platform with free high quality educational, technical training and capacity building content targeting Youth in Sudan and aiming to provide them with improved job skills to qualify them for better careers.

Why

The environment and institutional voids of the developing world can make it increasingly difficult to truly benefit from all of the educational content available online. This is due in part to the language barrier, the major economic environment and the issue of internet access that still plagues 69.1% of Sudan's population.

CivilEd's mission is to provide free high quality education to African Youths everywhere, enabling them to develop themselves and their communities. We want to end the inequality race, and we're starting with making education a right, rather than a privilege.

How

To make this a reality, we plan to do 5 simple things

  • We build our platform using Open EdX, an Open Source Software made by EdX.org and used by over dozens of universities and Organisations. This provides us with the best developed features, A Design studio for courses and complete Learning Management System (LMS) for the platform - For Free.
  • We curate free and available online educational content and translate them into local courses in local dialects and base them within local context. (i.e. Digital Marketing in Sudan, Graphic Designing through Smartphones, Program Management)
  • We partner with different entities such as Organisations that deliver workshops, company employee training programs, university courses and expert centered content. We help them in producing it as online courses or simply hosting it, in order to make it available to as many people, as possible and at any time, at no future costs.
  • We use mobile apps to allow users to download courses from our platform and even share them with other users through WiFi and hotspot capabilities. Guaranteeing access to users in low or no internet access regions through local focal points and peer-to-peer sharing.
  • By making our courses and programs free to audit, we ensure access for everyone. And by selling certificates for course completion at affordable prices, we help our users build up their résumés while supporting our partners for their content, incentivising more eductors to share content and ensuring the sustainability of our venture in our goal of reaching everyone.

The Open Box

Our most ambitious goal yet, The Open Box. Taking inspiration from The beekee box, We discovered the operation of the remote education device can be reproduced using The $45 Raspberry Pi computer chip and some open source software. With the total assembled cost of our device around $200, compared to the $6,000 Beekee Box. The Open Box stands to be portable, easy to use and a source for blended group learning anywhere at any time. This is our next step in ensuring quality education can be made available everywhere in Africa.
